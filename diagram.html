🫁 Pathway of Air

From Nostrils to Alveoli: The Journey of Oxygen

🔬 Alveoli: Gas Exchange Surface

Tiny air sacs surrounded by capillaries where oxygen enters the blood

📍 Trace the Pathway

Click on any step to learn more about each structure:

1 Nostrils (Nares)
The external openings where air enters. Hairs filter large particles.
2 Nasal Cavity
Air is warmed, moistened, and filtered by mucus and cilia.
3 Pharynx (Throat)
Shared pathway for air and food. Directs air toward the larynx.
4 Larynx (Voice Box)
Contains vocal cords. The epiglottis prevents food entry during swallowing.
5 Trachea (Windpipe)
Rigid tube supported by C-shaped cartilage rings, preventing collapse.
6 Bronchi
The trachea splits into two main bronchi, one to each lung.
7 Bronchioles
Smaller branches without cartilage, leading deeper into lung tissue.
8 Lungs
Spongy organs containing millions of alveoli. The left has 2 lobes, right has 3.
9 Alveoli
Microscopic air sacs (300 million per lung) where gas exchange occurs.

💡 Did You Know?

The respiratory system processes about 11,000 liters of air per day!
